## 1. Purpose

The Elosia Structured Distance Measurement (SDM) Framework is intended to support open scientific discussion, adversarial research exploration, and governed frontier experimentation — while preserving the integrity of the operational governance systems that make the framework structurally reliable.

This licensing framework establishes a clear separation between:

- the **Conceptual Research Layer**, which is released for public research participation and educational use, and
- the **Operational Governance Layer**, which remains proprietary to ElosiaEcosystem Inc.

The purpose of this separation is to encourage collaborative exploration of frontier reasoning systems without exposing or diluting the governance infrastructure responsible for provenance integrity, promotion control, and runtime enforcement.

## 2. The Conceptual Layer — Open Research Commons

The conceptual foundations of the SDM framework are released under the:

**Creative Commons Attribution–NonCommercial 4.0 International License (CC BY-NC 4.0)**

This layer is intended to function as an open cartographic instrument for the scientific commons.

### 2.1 Structured Distance Measurement (SDM)

The central thesis that the measured distance between a proposal and empirical validation is itself a durable and valuable research artifact. This includes:

- Evidence Gap analysis
- Empirical Gap analysis
- Logical Gap analysis
- Execution Gap analysis

### 2.2 The Tri-Agent Disruptor Exchange

The conceptual interaction model involving:

- Disruptor-1 (D1): Radical Proposer
- Disruptor-2 (D2): Dialectical Challenger
- Disruptor-3 (D3): Empirical Arbiter

Including adversarial paradigm separation, structured proposal exchange, arbitration logic, and validation-distance measurement.

### 2.3 The Consensus Trap Theory

The philosophical and structural argument that modern research systems frequently converge toward safe, incremental optimization while filtering out paradigm-level exploration. This includes adversarial reasoning principles, frontier cartography concepts, dissent preservation, and anti-convergence research structures.

### 2.4 Recursive Creativity Framework

The research framework describing historical patterns of innovation and knowledge expansion, including:

- Recombination of Known Tools
- Reinterpretation of Anomaly
- Constraint-Driven Refactoring (Bricolage)
- Systematic Elimination and Synthesis

## 6. Definition of the Operational Substrate

For the purposes of this framework, the term **"Operational Substrate"** refers to the runtime governance infrastructure responsible for:

- orchestration enforcement
- identity verification
- provenance integrity
- trust scoring
- memory governance
- promotion boundary control
- execution authorization
- forensic auditability

This layer represents the active governance engine of the framework.
